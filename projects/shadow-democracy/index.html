Shadow Democracy

Role Critical Thinker & Front-End Developer

Task Incite Reflection and Debate.

Tools Critical Thinking, HTML, CSS & JavaScript

Team Frank Roodnat, Kyllian Lockefeer & Lorenzo de Lijser

Assignment

This project was assigned by the academy, in the division: Void. We were assigned to create a speculative design. A design based on contemporary emerging technologies that questions the relationship between man and technology. The project has to incite reflection and debate.

Problem

People have no notion how much power we are giving to tech companies like Facebook by using their websites and apps. We are providing a lot of sensitive data to huge corporations without questioning it.

Our Goal

Our goal was to let people experience the capability of big datasets from big tech companies.

We created the voting system of the future. Instead of using pen and paper to cast your vote, you are required to log into your Facebook account. The new system will automatically generate a vote for you based on your personal user data.

Response

People reacted as expected. Some of the attendees did not want to login. And some of the attendees that did were astonished: "It just generated the right political party. How is this even possible?"

In actuality whenever pressing the login button a piece of JavaScript would generate a political party at random. The randomly generated vote got it right in some of the cases, which shocked the attendees.

Proposition

In the future, a law or a government would not be needed anymore because learning computers with smart algorithms based on digital information could take over these roles. They can search for specific data and find data patterns using these systems. With these systems, social media and digital technology can take over the democracy and government in the future. Your data will be influenced by the content you accumulate, thus creating a shadow democracy. Companies like Google and Facebook will be able to control the flow of their users and create their own network. A network able to access and control all social data.
