Stress Research

Role: Human-Centered Designer

Task: Gain Deeper Insights on Stress amongst High School Students

Tools: Context Mapping, Interviewing & a lot of Sticky Notes

Team: Five People

Assignment

This research project was assigned by the academy, in the division: Human. We were assigned to design a cultural probe to gain deeper insight into stress amongst high school students. A cultural probe is a participatory user self-documentation, containing various objects that are particularly designed to document the current situation of a research group.

The work of psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi inspired us at the start of the project. A lot of the information collected by interviewing the youngsters suited perfectly in the Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience by Csikszentmihalyi.

Process

After obtaining information by interviewing the high school students, we were up to design a cultural probe to gain even deeper insights into the stress that lives amongst these students. By using the Self-Determination Theory by Edward L. Deci and Richard Ryan, we had a better understanding of behavior. This information helped us to envision a frame to design parts for the cultural probe.

After designing and developing all the parts for the cultural probe, we were able to distribute it amongst our research group. The cultural probe contained short daily exercises to gain essential insights into the behavior of the high school students.

Results

We gathered all the results obtained from the exercises and ordered all of them. Patterns in the data provided insights into the behavior of the students. The most fascinating data we obtained, got re-visualized and presented. Some of the visualizations remained a degree of abstraction to be more inviting for interpretation.
